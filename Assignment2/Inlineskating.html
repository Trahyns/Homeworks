I first learned how to skate when I was in elementary school. I owned my first pair of quad skates!

My first experience going to Galaxy Skating Rink

I loved watching people of all ages gliding across the oval-shaped rink to music hits of that time. The entire atmosphere was fun! Being very eager to learn, I grabbed my first pair of quad skates and headed towards the rink.

When I got inside the skating rink:

  1. Quickly grabbed onto the wall
  2. My legs were wobbly, like a baby bird learning how to walk for the first time
  3. Bent my knees--following what I saw the other skaters doing
  4. Fell on my butt as soon as picked up some speed
  5. Got right back up again, and quickly became comfortable with skating

    When I first saw Inline Skates!

  • I was intriged
  • I was afraid
  • Thought to myself I was going to fall all over the place

It wasn't until after I made a new friend recently, I was motivated to purchase my first pair of inline skates. He mentioned how much fun it was to skate with his pair, and that I should really try them out.

With everything that is happening in the world today--COVID-19, forcing everyone to quarantine. I really was looking forward to learning a new hobbie. Especially, since I was stuck working from home, sitting behind a desk all day 5 days a week. I could'nt wait to try them for a spin!

My parents wished me good luck, when I left to meet with my friend to learn how to skate. At first step, I felt like I was going to roll away and never stop. I had got the balance down packed, but trying to turn was a bit tricky. I almost fell numerous times too! Hahaha! But luckily, I had my good friend by my side to catch me.

I had no safety gear at the moment, except for wrist guards. I promised my dad I would invest in a helmet, along with knee and elbow pads.

I was told after my skating session that I did a great job! Didn't fall, and held my balance. Whew! that was a true core workout! I am definitely looking forward to skating again.
